Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about quoting, products, shipping, and installation.

Quotes & Lead Times

How do I request a quote for multiple products?

Use our Start a Project form to request pricing for multiple items at once. You can add products to your cart and submit them together, or describe your full project scope in the form. We'll review your request and respond with a consolidated quote.

What details help you quote faster?

The more specific you can be, the faster we can respond with accurate pricing:

  • • Product categories and quantities
  • • Preferred dimensions (width, depth, height)
  • • Material preferences (steel, wood, phenolic, etc.)
  • • Any special requirements (ADA compliance, seismic, etc.)
  • • Delivery location and timeline

If you're not sure about specifics, that's okay—we'll ask clarifying questions.

How do lead times vary for standard vs configured requests?

Fast-Ship items: Ships within 5–10 business days. These are pre-configured products with set specifications.

Standard configurations: 4–6 weeks typical lead time. This includes most casework, benches, and fume hoods with common options.

Custom or complex projects: 6–10 weeks depending on scope. Projects requiring non-standard dimensions, special finishes, or extensive coordination take additional time.

We'll confirm lead time in your quote based on current production schedules.

Shipping & Receiving

What should I do if the freight arrives damaged?

If you notice damage upon delivery:

  1. 1. Document it immediately. Take photos of all damage to packaging and products before signing.
  2. 2. Note damage on the BOL. Write "damaged" or "possible damage" on the bill of lading and have the driver sign.
  3. 3. Contact us within 48 hours. Email support@iq-laboratory.com with your order number and photos.

Do not refuse delivery unless the damage is severe. We can file a freight claim and arrange replacement parts faster if you accept the shipment.

How do I prepare for delivery/receiving?

Before your shipment arrives:

  • • Confirm delivery access (dock height, door width, forklift availability)
  • • Clear the path to the installation area
  • • Assign someone to receive and inspect the shipment
  • • Have the order confirmation ready to verify quantities

Most shipments arrive on LTL freight trucks. You'll receive tracking information and an estimated delivery window before arrival.

Can you ship using our carrier account?

Yes. If you have a preferred carrier or freight account, let us know when requesting your quote. We can ship on your account for most domestic orders. Provide your carrier name and account number, and we'll coordinate pickup.

Installation & Assembly

Do products arrive assembled or knocked down?

It depends on the product:

  • • Fume hoods: Ship fully assembled (superstructure). Base cabinets ship separately.
  • • Casework: Ships knocked down (KD) for efficient shipping. Requires on-site assembly.
  • • Tables & benches: Typically ship knocked down. Legs, frames, and tops ship separately with hardware.
  • • Storage cabinets: Most ship fully assembled.

Assembly instructions are included with each shipment. See our Resources page for downloadable manuals.

What does IQ need to confirm before installation?

For fume hoods and connected equipment, we'll need to verify:

  • • Duct location and size
  • • Electrical service location (voltage, phase, amperage)
  • • Plumbing rough-in locations (if applicable)
  • • Ceiling height and clearance requirements

For casework installations, confirm wall blocking locations and floor conditions. We provide submittal drawings for review before manufacturing.

Where do I find manuals and installation instructions?

Visit our Resources page to download cut sheets, installation manuals, and CAD files by product series. You can search by product name or browse by category.

Product Selection & Compatibility

How do I choose between base, wall, and floor cabinets?

Base cabinets sit on the floor and support countertops. Standard heights are 35" (with adjustable leveling feet). Standard depths are 22" or 24".

Wall cabinets mount above counters or workbenches. Standard depths are 12"–14" to clear users standing at the counter.

Tall/floor cabinets are full-height storage units (typically 84"–90" tall). Use these for supplies, equipment, or specialized storage like acid/flammable cabinets.

Not sure what combination works for your space? Start a project request and we'll help you plan.

What's the difference between acid and flammable storage cabinets?

Acid/corrosive cabinets are designed for storing corrosive chemicals. They feature:

  • • Corrosion-resistant polyethylene or epoxy-coated steel construction
  • • Ventilation provisions for fume control
  • • Separate storage from flammables

Flammable storage cabinets are designed for flammable liquids per OSHA and NFPA 30. They feature:

  • • Double-wall steel construction with air gap
  • • Self-closing doors (required in most jurisdictions)
  • • Liquid-tight sump at bottom

Note: Always confirm requirements with your local fire marshal and safety officer. Code requirements vary by jurisdiction.

When should I choose a bench vs a table?

Lab tables are standalone work surfaces with open space underneath. Choose tables when you need:

  • • Flexibility to move or reconfigure
  • • Knee clearance for seated work
  • • Equipment access from multiple sides

Lab benches combine work surfaces with integrated storage (drawers, cabinets, shelving). Choose benches when you need:

  • • Built-in storage at the workstation
  • • A more permanent installation
  • • Utility integration (power, gas, vacuum)

How do I choose the right fume hood type?

Bench-top fume hoods are the most common. They sit on a base cabinet or support structure at standard counter height (36").

Floor-mounted (walk-in) fume hoods provide full floor-to-ceiling access. Use these for large apparatus or equipment that won't fit in a standard hood.

Specialty hoods include:

  • • Perchloric acid hoods: Stainless steel with integral wash-down system
  • • Radioisotope hoods: Seamless stainless interior for decontamination
  • • High-performance hoods: Low-flow designs for energy savings

Need help selecting? Include your application in your project request and we'll recommend the right type.

Fume Hood Technical

What standards apply to fume hoods, and what do they mean?

ASHRAE 110 is the industry standard for testing fume hood containment. It measures how well the hood captures and contains fumes under various conditions. A hood that passes ASHRAE 110 testing has demonstrated effective containment at the specified face velocity.

SEFA 1 (Scientific Equipment and Furniture Association) defines recommended practices for fume hood design, construction, and testing. It covers materials, dimensions, and performance criteria.

iQ Labs fume hoods are tested to ASHRAE 110 and manufactured to SEFA 1 guidelines. Test reports are available on our Resources page.

What information is needed for duct/exhaust coordination?

Your HVAC contractor or engineer will need:

  • • Exhaust collar size: Typically 8", 10", or 12" round depending on hood width
  • • CFM requirements: Based on hood size and required face velocity
  • • Static pressure: For fan selection and duct sizing
  • • Collar location: Top exhaust (standard) or rear exhaust (for low ceilings)

We provide this information on our cut sheets and can coordinate directly with your mechanical engineer during the submittal process.

What are sash configurations and why do they matter?

The sash is the movable glass front of the fume hood. Configuration affects both safety and energy use:

Vertical rising sash: Standard configuration. Sash moves straight up and down. Provides full opening width when raised.

Combination sash: Vertical sash with horizontal sliding panels. Allows users to work with arms inside while maintaining lower sash height—reducing exhaust volume and energy costs.

Horizontal sliding sash: Side-to-side panels only. Good for constant-volume systems where face velocity must stay consistent.

For VAV (variable air volume) systems, combination sashes are often preferred because they allow users to keep the sash low while still accessing the interior, which reduces energy consumption.

When are explosion-proof fixtures required?

Explosion-proof (XP) electrical fixtures are required when working with flammable vapors that could accumulate inside the hood. This includes:

  • • Work with volatile solvents (acetone, ethanol, toluene, etc.)
  • • Processes that generate flammable gas
  • • Storage of flammable materials inside the hood

Standard hoods with non-XP fixtures are appropriate for most applications where flammable materials are used in small quantities with proper ventilation.

Note: Consult your safety officer and local code requirements. iQ Labs offers explosion-proof electrical packages as an option on all fume hood models.

Warranty & Service

What warranty coverage do iQ Labs products include?

iQ Labs products are covered by our standard warranty:

  • • Casework & cabinets: 10-year limited warranty on structure and finish
  • • Fume hoods: 5-year limited warranty on superstructure; 1-year on electrical and mechanical components
  • • Tables & benches: 5-year limited warranty on frames and structures
  • • Countertops: Varies by material (contact us for specifics)

Warranty covers defects in materials and workmanship under normal use. See your order documentation for complete warranty terms.

How do I request support for a warranty or service issue?

Contact our support team at support@iq-laboratory.com. To help us resolve your issue quickly, please include:

  • • Your order number or PO number
  • • Product name and model (from the label or original quote)
  • • Description of the issue
  • • Photos showing the problem

We typically respond within 1 business day. For urgent issues, call 616-583-8876 during business hours (Mon–Fri, 8am–5pm CT).
